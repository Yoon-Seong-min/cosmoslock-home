Core Technology

üí° Core Mechanism

Mutual verification of IoT devices based on communication history

Limitations of conventional IoT authentication: Passwords, certificates, and biometrics can create concentration risk when a single credential or authority becomes a primary control point. In compromise scenarios, this may enable broader lateral movement.

NexAuth for IoT innovation: Designed to use peer-reviewed communication history as an additional verification signal. Devices and access requests can be evaluated against configurable thresholds (e.g., network contact coverage over a defined observation window) to flag unfamiliar or anomalous entities for blocking or step-up approval.

Key idea: ‚Äúcollective-witness authentication,‚Äù where peer devices contribute evidence signals for verification decisions (subject to site-specific policy).
